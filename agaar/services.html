Mini-Grid Systems

A small-scale power network fueled by solar energy is known as a mini-grid. The clients linked to this mini-grid power network get the generated electricity either directly or indirectly through batteries. It is simple to link a group of people that live close to one another, such as in a hamlet, to the grid. In Kenya's underserved rural areas, where sunlight is available for most of the year, solar mini-grids are the most flexible and practical method for supplying electricity. First off, Kenya's policy landscape is becoming more pro-renewable energy. Solar photovoltaic (PV) technology's cost and accessibility have both continuously improved over the past ten years, and unlike just a few years ago, solar power systems are now modular and expandable based on the needs of the customers. A solar mini-grid has the benefit of being able to handle increased energy demands, such as those of Kenyan enterprises. Kenya's rural areas benefit from a solar mini-grid, but it can only be economically implemented if clients are close enough to one another.
